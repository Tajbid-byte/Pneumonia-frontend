Classification

Binary Screening Classes

PneuXNet performs binary classification distinguishing normal lung fields from pneumonia-consistent radiographic findings.

CLASS 0
Normal
ICD-10: Z03.89

Clear lung fields bilaterally. No consolidation, infiltrates, or pleural effusion. Normal cardiac silhouette. Diaphragmatic contours intact. Costophrenic angles sharp and well-defined.

CLASS 1
Pneumonia
ICD-10: J18.9

Focal consolidation or ground-glass opacification in one or more lung zones. Increased opacity consistent with airspace disease. Air bronchograms or pleural effusion may be present. Immediate clinical correlation required.

Methodology

Novel Architecture

PneuXNet introduces two key innovations on a DenseNet201 backbone, achieving superior accuracy with full XAI interpretability.

01
🔷
DenseNet201 Backbone

Pre-trained on ImageNet with dense connectivity patterns. Replaced final block for domain adaptation. Feeds parallel atrous convolution streams at dilation rates 1, 3, and 5.

02
⬡
MSAA Module

Multi-Scale Atrous Attention captures subtle pathological features at multiple receptive fields. Channel and spatial attention (Squeeze-and-Excitation) refine feature maps, forcing focus on diagnostically salient regions.

03
◈
CAAP Head

Class-Aware Adaptive Pooling prevents spatial information dilution from standard global average pooling. Generates adaptive weights via a lightweight MLP on class probabilities, producing a weighted spatial attention map for the final feature vector.
